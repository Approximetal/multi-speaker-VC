Audio Samples from "Cross-Lingual Many-to-Many Voice Conversion by Disentangling Speaker Characteristics from Speech Content"

Authors:

Zhiyuan Zhao, Ruotong Wang, Luoyu Bai, Bai Li, Wan Ding, Dongyan Huang

Abstract:

Cross-lingual many-to-many voice conversion (VC) is a challenging task due to the pronunciation error issues and the lack of parallel bilingual dataset. In this paper, we propose a cross-lingual many-to-many voice conversion model, consisting of a VC encoder to extract speech content, a speaker encoder to verify speaker characteristics, and a decoder with attention to synthesize personalized speech. During training process, we employ a self-conversion training strategy to make the attention model more robust for alignment. Then we construct one-to-many training system, which takes parallel TTS synthesized speech and multi-speaker natural speech signals, to disentangle speaker characteristics from speech content, thus reduce pronunciation errors. Finally, based on one-to-many pre-trained model, we explore many-to-many model using VCC 2020 dataset to realize cross-lingual VC, i.e., English, Mandarin, Germany and Finnish. The proposed approach does not need extra labels, parallel bilingual dataset, or auxiliary modules such as Connectionist Temporal Classification (CTC), Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR), and Variational Auto-Encoder (VAE). Our experimental results show the proposed approach significantly improves naturalness and similarity of converted speech , which close to human's voice.
